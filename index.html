Benefit Appeal in Live Streaming:

The Moderating Role of Influencers’ Beauty and Body Motion Magnitude

ABSTRACT

Benefit appeal is one of the most commonly used advertising tactics for improving consumer response. However, in live-streaming shopping, which featured the new multimedia, real-time, and entertainment nature, how benefit appeals work has not yet been confirmed. To address this question, this article developed a three-step empirical research with 10,971 live-stream videos and machine-learning methods. Study 1 employed interpretable machine learning methods, using a predictive framework to analyze the importance of benefit appeals on sales. Study 2 utilized propensity score matching (PSM) to explore the quasi-causal relationship between benefit appeals and sales. Results demonstrated that benefit appeals significantly enhance sales, with influencers’ facial beauty and body motion amplifying this effect as mediators. Study 3 confirmed these findings through experimental validation, ensuring the reliability and validity of the causal relationship between benefit appeals and sales. Thus, this empirical multimodal study enriches the literature on benefit appeals in the context of multimedia live-streaming shopping.

1.2 Example of beauty score detection API provided by Face++

The evaluation of influencer's beauty is conducted through a beauty score detection API from Face++. This tool utilizes 83 detailed facial landmarks to accurately delineate the face's contours, such as those of the eyes, eyebrows, lips, and nose. Leveraging these landmarks, Face++ predicts the beauty score. Figure 2 show the facial landmarks and Face++'s beauty score detection interface. ​

1.3 Example of dense optical flow algorithm

The body motion magnitude is obtained through the dense optical flow algorithm. By analyzing two adjacent frames of a live-streaming video, this algorithm locates the influencer’s body position and captures the vector of body movement (see Figure 3). Video 1 demonstrates an example of dense optical flow, with the intensity of the red lines on the right indicating the magnitude of the influencer’s body motion.
